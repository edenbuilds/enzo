# Method and provenance

## Heuristics

- Focus is a sequence of exclusions, not a longer priority list.
- The first promise must be legible in the product experience, not only in copy.
- A coherent whole is more useful than locally excellent fragments.

## Diagnostic questions

What user transformation is singular? Which feature, message, or workflow dilutes it? What observable behavior would disprove the promise?

## Exclusions and blind spots

Do not infer modern market facts from historical speeches. Do not use this lens for finance, law, or claims about what Jobs would say. Product intuition must be checked against current customer evidence.

## Modern transfer

Enzo applies focus and coherence as an analytical method, then tests conclusions against current evidence. It does not copy prose, presentation style, or product aesthetics.
