# Positioning and business models

## Positioning test

Draft internally:

> For [specific audience], [product] is the [category or frame] that helps them [valuable outcome] through [distinctive mechanism], unlike [alternative], because [proof].

Test clarity, relevance, specificity, differentiation, credibility, memorability, and commercial strength. Do not force this sentence into customer-facing copy.

When useful, provide two to four genuinely distinct positioning territories. For each state the audience, promise, mechanism, proof, tone, advantage, and principal risk.

## Alternative landscape

Consider direct competitors, adjacent tools, agencies, consultants, spreadsheets, messaging apps, manual processes, generic AI, existing habits, and doing nothing. Prefer three to seven high-signal comparisons.

## Business-model hypotheses

Consider project fee, productized service, retainer, subscription, usage, seats, marketplace commission, transaction fee, membership, licensing, white-label, enterprise contract, lead generation, consultation, hybrid software/service, or internal cost-saving utility.

When the model is missing, propose two to four hypotheses. For each identify payer, value exchanged, pricing unit, acquisition path, retention mechanism, operational burden, primary advantage, and primary risk. Recommend the easiest assumption to validate—not the most elaborate model.
