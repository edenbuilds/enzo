# Adaptive question bank

Select questions; do not administer this as a fixed questionnaire.

## Purpose and success

- What must this experience achieve first: qualified demand, comprehension, trust, activation, retention, efficiency, reputation, or validation?
- Which result would prove the direction works: conversion, lead quality, activation, retention, task completion, time saved, adoption, or maintenance cost?
- Is this commercial, client, internal, personal, or exploratory?

## Audience and position

- Who feels the problem most acutely?
- Which posture fits: premium specialist, category challenger, technical authority, friendly guide, fast and accessible, or quietly superior utility?
- What should people choose this for: outcomes, speed, simplicity, expertise, trust, control, privacy, design, workflow, or service?

## Brand and emotional direction

- Choose up to three traits: authoritative, warm, rigorous, provocative, calm, inventive, human, precise, optimistic, or discreet.
- What should the hero make the audience feel: understood, challenged, reassured, energized, curious, or in control?
- Which traits must be avoided?

## Visual direction

- Select a territory: editorial, technical, cinematic, utilitarian, expressive, institutional, product-led, or minimal.
- Choose density: sparse, balanced, compact, or data-dense.
- Choose motion: nearly static, functional, expressive, or immersive.

## Product and journey

- What must a new visitor understand before acting?
- Which proof matters most: outcomes, customer evidence, methodology, product demonstration, credentials, security, or transparent limitations?
- Which action is primary?
- What can be deferred from the first release?

## Contradiction checks

Surface, do not silently resolve:

- Premium specialist versus lowest-price acquisition
- Minimal presentation versus dense content requirements
- Broad audience versus specialist messaging
- Fast MVP versus extensive custom interaction
- Original identity versus close visual recreation
- Strong privacy posture versus unnecessary third-party dependencies
- Personal-use scope versus enterprise-grade operations
