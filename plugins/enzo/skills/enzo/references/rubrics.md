# Experience audit rubrics

## Marketing and landing pages

Check first-screen comprehension, audience specificity, problem framing, value proposition, proof, objection handling, CTA continuity, pricing or engagement logic, navigation depth, SEO signals, and mobile hierarchy.

## Product-led websites

Check product demonstration, feature-to-outcome translation, use cases, integration story, activation path, plan boundaries, switching motivation, and credibility of claims.

## Web applications and SaaS

Check navigation model, task completion, empty/loading/error states, onboarding, progressive disclosure, state visibility, recovery, density, keyboard access, and consistency across core flows.

## AI products

Check what the model does, input/output expectations, uncertainty, human control, failure recovery, privacy, data handling, evaluation proof, latency expectations, and whether automation claims exceed observable capability.

## Ecommerce

Check discovery, filtering, product comprehension, merchandising, trust, shipping/returns, cart continuity, checkout friction, responsive shopping, and post-purchase clarity.

## Portfolios and personal brands

Check identity, specialization, quality of evidence, case-study narrative, role clarity, credibility, contact path, and whether the work, not decoration, carries the argument.

## Internal and personal tools

Check frequency of use, workflow fit, privacy, speed, failure tolerance, maintenance burden, manual alternatives, data ownership, backup, and export. Treat acquisition and monetization as optional.

## Codebase-aware pass

Inspect routes, page/layout components, tokens, typography, navigation, content sources, analytics, forms, metadata, breakpoints, animation, accessibility, and state handling. Keep implementation findings distinct from strategic and visual findings.
