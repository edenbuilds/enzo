# Scoring and evidence

## Finding contract

Each material finding should contain:

- Category and concise title
- Direct observation
- Strategic or user consequence
- Recommended action
- Severity: low, medium, high, or critical
- Confidence from 0 to 1
- Evidence citation or `inference: true`
- Approximate impact and effort

## Confidence guide

- `0.90–1.00`: Repeated, directly observed evidence across relevant states.
- `0.75–0.89`: Clear observation with a well-supported consequence.
- `0.55–0.74`: Partial evidence or a consequence that still requires validation.
- `<0.55`: Hypothesis only; do not present as a finding without a coverage warning.

## Score dimensions

Use a 1–10 scale only when comparative clarity matters: clarity, positioning, content, information architecture, visual hierarchy, brand coherence, differentiation, product comprehension, conversion, trust, mobile readiness, accessibility, and implementation quality.

- 1–3: Foundational weakness
- 4–5: Functional but materially weak
- 6: Adequate
- 7: Strong with visible gaps
- 8: Highly effective
- 9: Exceptional
- 10: Category-defining and supported by unusually strong evidence

Never reduce the audit to one unexplained overall score. Include coverage alongside scoring.

## Coverage gaps

Name what was unavailable: authenticated flows, mobile states, analytics, private repository, performance data, user research, pricing, or business context. Explain how each gap limits the conclusion and what smallest artifact would close it.
